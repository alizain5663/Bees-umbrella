Umbrella Company vs. Limited Company: Pros and Cons Summary

Tax Efficiency

LIMITED

The most tax-efficient method of contracting. The majority of directors work for a low income. Dividends are not subject to National Insurance, and other tax planning alternatives are available.

Umbrella

PAYE taxes your entire salary. Working through a limited business is less tax efficient.

Ease of set up and use

Company incorporation can be accomplished in a few hours, but tax registration (VAT, Corporation Tax) can take several weeks. Monthly accounting administration is essential, as are some annual requirements as a company director.

Instant setup, minimal administration, and no legal obligations.

IR35 Consideration

Excellent if you intend to serve several clients. The possibility of being blanketed. If caught, the majority of the tax benefits of being limited are forfeited.

Because you are already taxed as a 'employee,' IR35 is irrelevant. Not great if you intend to work for multiple clients concurrently.

Duration

Due of the administrative work required to set up and then shut the company, it is not suitable for short-term contracts.

Excellent for short-term contracts.

Low earning contracts

Contracts paying less than £15-20/hour are usually not suggested.

Excellent for lower-paying contracts.

How Does Umbrella Company Work

STEP 1

The umbrella firm (as your 'employer') signs a contract with your recruitment agency once you have acquired a contract role.

STEP 2

You will also sign an employment contract with the umbrella.

STEP 3

After completing a pre-agreed-upon time period, you complete a timesheet and have it signed by your management before sending it to both your recruitment agency and the umbrella firm.

STEP 4

The umbrella company will bill the recruitment agency, which will then bill the end-client.

STEP 5

Once the umbrella company receives payment from the agency, it will process your payroll and pay you a salary after deducting employment taxes, the pre-agreed umbrella fee/margin, personal taxes, and pension contributions (if applicable). Other benefits of using an umbrella include same-day setup and the provision of some statutory benefits such as sick pay, holiday pay, and employment protection. This provides contractors with a quick and efficient way to get paid, as well as some job security. However, the most significant benefit of contracting through an umbrella, and one that explains their growing popularity, is that using one completely eliminates the risk posed by IR35.
